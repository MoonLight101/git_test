HTML Entities

There are three reserved characters in HTML: < > and &. You should always use HTML entities for these three characters.
Entities always begin with an ampersand (&) and end with a semicolon (;).
In between, you put a special code that your browser will interpret as a symbol.

If you’re into “web typography,” you’ll also find yourself using curly quotes quite a bit.

There's four different kinds of curly quotes (opening and closing single and double quotes):

  • “ - &ldquo;
  • ” - &rdquo;
  • ‘ - &lsquo;
  • ’ - &rsquo;
